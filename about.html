Bengie was a scaredy dog,
As skittish as I ever found,
Be it of a sudden noise,
Or a rotisserie going round,

He feared the mice in the barn,
And the cat atop the shed,
The icy hail falling down,
And the lightning over head,

His biggest fear was of cars,
Seeing sharp teeth in their grills,
Evil eyes in their head lights,
And vicious claws in their wheels,

Once when out on a walk,
Another a dog chose to fight,
Adding to his fearful life,
Ever since that night,

Other dogs seemed oh so large,
And really, really loud,
While they played out in the park,
He' rather watch the clouds,

One exception though there was,
His bestest friend alive,
A beautiful calm brown labrdor,
Behind whom he could hide,

She saw nothing to fear anywhere,
And thought everyone a friend,
She helped him join in all the fun,
Tails wagging end to end,

And as he grew he'd start to see,
The word through her calm eyes,
With her love snd encouragement,
Though only when he tried,

She'd push him every single day,
To face one of his fears,
Always there to comfort him,
Through the smiles and through the tears,

At first he'd need her right there,
Right there, right by his side,
But each day got a tad better,
So she could step aside,

One day once he got quiet big,
All his old fears seemed so small,
As looking back upon it now,
Life is not so scary after all
